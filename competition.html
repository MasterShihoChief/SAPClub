It’s time for a major upgrade!

Ever thought Glenn Hall needed a coffee shop? Want a stream running through the building? Do you think you can design a more effective classroom or lab?

In the near future, Glenn Hall is going to be torn down and rebuilt. The design competition is a way for students within the business organizations to leave their mark on Chico State. From the classrooms and labs, to the look of the building itself, the students have the power. Students will have free reign to create designs and imagine new ways for the business building to look and function.

The new building is going to be here for a while, so think to the future and have your designs be adaptable to future technologies and teaching styles. For example, we may no longer have computers in classrooms, as classes may be set up for BYOD (bring your own device).

There are multiple categories in which organizations may compete. The various types, sizes, and layouts are to be dictated by the designers. Each design should be sustainable. Some categories have specific requirements while others are open to interpretation.

Collaboration Classrooms:

Classrooms designed for classes that need the capability to run technology. Remember technology in the future can and will change so these classrooms need to be designed to be able to accommodate future technological advancements. These classrooms are for innovation and hands on experience for students.

Requirements:

  • Accommodate 50+ individuals at any given time.
  • Provide storage for education and training materials.
  • Collaboration areas - Technology and Innovation stations.
  • Provide storage for supplies.

Lecture Classrooms:

Classrooms designed for classes that need the capability to run technology. Remember technology in the future can and will change so these classrooms need to be designed to be able to accommodate future technological advancements. These classrooms are for innovation and hands on experience for students.

Requirements:

  • Accommodate 50+ individuals at any given time.
  • Provide storage for education and training materials.
  • Projection/audio-visual equipment.

Computer/Technology Lab:

This is a lab for students who may not have the resources to do classwork off-campus.

Requirements:

  • Accommodate 50-75 individuals at any given time.
  • Provide for storage.
  • Technology stations and equipment.

Communal Areas:

These designs can be areas either indoor or outdoor where students can lounge or study. They can be break rooms where students could get a bite to eat or even coffee shops. The communal category is for any place outside of classes where students can interact within a comfortable environment.

Requirements:

  • Accommodate 20+ individuals at any given time.

Tutoring Labs:

The Student Success Center in Glenn Hall is our business tutoring center; tutoring center will be needed within the new building. This area is for people who want to create a fun and relaxing environment where students want to come to get the help they need for their classes. Come up with unique ways to set up a tutoring center that allows efficient tutoring of all business subjects.

Requirements:

  • Accommodate 50+ individuals at a given time.
  • Provide storage for education and training materials.
  • Projection/audio-visual equipment.

Exterior Design:

This category is for teams wishing to design the exterior look of the new business building. These designs must follow firecode.

Outdoor Areas:

Any areas outside surrounding the building, such as gardens, seating areas, tables, etc. This is an area where you can really let your imagination run wild. Come up with fun and unique ways to upgrade the surrounding area of the new business building.

Miscellaneous (Open Category):

A room design that does not quite fit any of the categories mentioned above. (ie: faculty offices, a new entrepreneurship center, or a trading center)

The Guidelines:

  • The competition is open to all College of Business clubs and organizations.
  • Clubs and organizations may have up to 3 teams consisting of 2 - 4 people each.
  • A team is limited to one entry in one category.
  • If there are multiple teams, each team must choose a separate category.
  • Club members can only participate on one team. If they are in multiple clubs they may participate on two different club teams. For example, someone may be on an SAP team and on a fraternity’s team, but they cannot be on two SAP teams.
  • Teams will need to register no later than March 10th.
  • Teams have the option to request an engineering student for the competition.
    • The American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE) is joining us to provide civil engineering students to Glenn 2.0 teams to help assist with the creation of designs, help with sustainability, and help follow code regulations.
    • Engineering students are optional and not a requirement.
  • Each entry needs to have AT LEAST ONE sustainability component to their design.
    • Design(size)
    • Durability
    • Energy Efficiency
    • Waste Reduction
    • Indoor Air Quality
    • Water Conservation
    • Green Products

First Round:

  • Teams will create a single 20”x30” poster or model that best captures their design.
  • Designs will be presented on April 14th, during our Glenn 2.0 Design Gala which will be open to all students within the College of Business. Teams should be prepared to answer questions regarding the intent of the design, the advantages of the space, and the improvement of the learning environment.
  • The Top Ten teams will be chosen by popular vote of the attending students and faculty.

Second Round:

  • Top Ten teams will need to prepare a maximum of 5 slides to be submitted by April 18th to cobglenn2.0@gmail.com.
  • Teams will need to prepare a 2-5 minute pitch, presented at an event on April 20th, open to all students within the College of Business.

Winners:

Winners will then be chosen by popular vote of the attending students and faculty.

Cash prizes for the organizations will be given as follows:

  • First Place: $500
  • Second Place: $300
  • Third Place: $200

Top teams’ members will be presented with Amazon gift-cards.

Round 1 Presentation:

Each team will need to create a visual representation of their design in the form of a poster (20” x 30”) or a model. The poster will be presented to the College of Business student body and faculty in a gallery setting. The top ten teams will be decided by a majority vote. Each team needs to email their decision of creating a poster or model to cobglenn2.0@gmail.com by April 10th.

Round 2 Presentation:

Teams will have 2-5 minutes to pitch their designs, which should touch on key features of their design, comparison to the current building, and explanation of effective improvements. There is no restriction on software use for the presentation (Prezi, Powerpoint, etc.). Order of presenters will be randomly selected.

Important Dates:

  • March 10th - Registration Due
  • April 10th - Inform if you are creating a model or poster for Gala.
  • April 12th - Glenn 2.0 Design Gala
  • April 18th - Round 2 Presentation Slide Due
  • April 19th - Presentation Roster Announced
  • April 20th - Round 2 Presentation

**All dates are subject to change. Team leaders will be informed of changes within a timely manner.
