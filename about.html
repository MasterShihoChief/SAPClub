The SAP Club is a student run organization under Chico State’s College of Business, Information Systems program. Our organization offers its members the most current SAP Business Suite software’s and resources. These tools allow our members to develop the foundational skill sets necessary to excel in the business environment. Each semester we provide our students with different SAP ERP modules to diversify their experience.

  • SAP ERP Module Examples:
    • S/4 Hana
    • Production Planning
    • Personas
    • SAP SRM 7.0 (Supplier Relationship Management)
    • SAP FICO (Financial and Control document)
    • Customer Relationship Management
    • ABAP Programming

Club members also gain professional experience through local and national competitions as well as through working relationships with our industry partners by addressing their current real-world business problems.

The SAP Club was started in the Fall of 2011 by a group of students with the desire to further their SAP skill set. While the classroom and instructional environment provided a solid knowledge base, these students wanted to drastically improve their skills and knowledge of all SAP Business Suites. SAP club members immediately began attending SAP sponsored events and competing against big name schools like Berkley, Sanford, and Notre Dame. Throughout these events, SAP Club members have proven to be outstanding leaders as well as problem solvers in every competition we’ve competed in.

In the Fall of 2016, the SAP Club partnered with both the University Alliance and the Chico State SAP Competence Center to lay the foundation for NextGen Consulting. This student run consulting firm mutually benefits students and our industry partners by challenging our club members with real-world problem solving as well as an opportunity to demonstrate their SAP skillset in a professional environment.

  • California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation
    • Developing a business solution for one of their in-house SAP Modules using Personas.
  • NextGen
    • Working with UCC Director Tom Wilder in establishing a professional IT Consulting experience for members.
    • Our goal is to leverage talent from both the SAP Club & BIS Society members against real world business problems.
  • BISSOP
    • Effective Spring 2017, the BIS Student Outreach Program will be responsible for recruiting and educating prospective Information Systems students from local community colleges and high schools.
    • Our primary objective is to recruit and train students in order to better address the demands of our industry partners.
